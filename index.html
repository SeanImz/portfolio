About Me

I'm a career changer that worked for many years as a biologist and research scientist before moving into a management job that involved some web development.

Now I've been bitten by the development bug, which has led me to acquire many new skills; but I feel an unquenchable thirst to learn more.

I'm seeking an opportunity to apply and expand my skills and experience in an environment that encourages individual contribution and growth to benefit the whole team and organisation.

I originally learned to program as a teenager, using BASIC to write a database program and some simple games.

Other things
I like to do...

I'm also an avid reader and book collector, nature lover, wildlife photographer, rock music fan, greyhound walker, exotic gardener and tropical fish enthusiast.

Why
Mudskipper_dev?

Mudskippers are fascinating fishes that live in the transitional zone between land and water, and they are an important evolutionary link between aquatic and terrestrial animals.

As a biologist "evolving" into a web developer, they also seemed like the perfect metaphor...

Living in this transitional zone makes Mudskippers resilient and adaptable - qualities I would like to emulate on my own transition into coding!
